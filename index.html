Research Spotlight

Research Spotlight

The highlight of the quarter on the research side is that our research operating model is closing the loop between thesis and investment in the same period. Dani London and Caroline Broder published our most substantive dive of Q1, Insurance: Agentic Back-Office for Carriers and Brokers (ade-b10.github.io/insurance-research). The one-line argument: insurance is one of the last major unconnected financial data ecosystems, and the first company to win the connectivity layer becomes the system of record for insurance data and the distribution layer for everything downstream — verification, monitoring, underwriting signals, and eventually brokerage.

That dive was not an academic exercise. It was the spine of a Series A we led in the same quarter, and the founder told us directly that the memo was the deciding factor in choosing Base10 over a competing term sheet. This is the shape we want the research function to have — published thesis, diligence through the lens of the thesis, investment made, repeat — and it is how we expect the firm's research output to compound returns over the coming years.

Two other pieces are in flight this quarter and will be covered in the next letter: a companion memo on AI Agent Insurance & Certification (link) and a survey of Voice AI infrastructure (link) mapping to several existing portfolio positions.
